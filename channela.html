CHANNEL A: INCENTIVE EFFECTS

Previous studies have shown that Prospera has increased investments in human capital of poor children. Evidence from other contexts suggests that investment in children improves their outcomes later in life.

We verified this intuition by assessing the relationship between years of education and job outcomes for the youth . Our results indicate that one additional year of schooling is associated with a 5.3% increase in monthly salary of the respondentâ€™s first and current job when controlling for gender of the youth, whether the youth is currently in school, years of education for the youth, whether the youth has ever migrated to the US or within Mexico, and state fixed effects. This relationship is significant at the 1% level. There is also a positive and statistically significant relationship between years of schooling and probability of formal employment.
