CHANNEL A: INCENTIVE EFFECTS

Previous studies have shown that Prospera increases investments in nutrition, health and schooling, of poor children. Evidence from other contexts suggests that these types of investments improve their outcomes later in life.

This study verified this intuition by assessing the relationship between years of education and young adults job market outcomes. The results indicate that one additional year of schooling is associated with a 5.3% increase in monthly salary of young adultsâ€™s first and current (at time data was collected) jobs when controlling for gender, if the responent was currently in school, years of education, if the youth ever migrated to the US or within Mexico, and the state the youth was from. This relationship is significant at the 1% level. There is also a positive and statistically significant relationship between years of schooling and probability that a young adult has a formal employment contract.
