CHANNEL B: INCOME EFFECTS

The amount of transfers that accrue to a household while a beneficiary is enrolled in the program varies with the birth years and age differences between the youth of interest and their older siblings. The age structure of the older siblings of Prospera beneficiary youth in this cohort is not affected by the program itself, and does not affect labor market outcomes later in life.

Therefore, we are able to exploit this feature of the program to measure how additional transfers impact wages and probability of formal employment. We find that a 1% increase in transfers increase salary of first job by .86% and salary of current job by .95%. This finding is significant at the 5% level.

Additionally, a 1% increases probability of formal employment in first job .14% and of current job by .19%. These findings are significant at the 1% level. We do not find any affect of additional transfers on probability of being employed.
