CHANNEL C: SUPPLEMENTAL EFFECTS

Three categories of supplemental programs â€“ entrepreneurship support programs, job matching programs, and job training and mentorship programs, have been piloted or considered by Prospera. Though some evidence from the literature and survey of graduates suggests these three approaches may be useful, implementation constraints and difficulty of targeting youth after they leave the program make supplemental interventions infeasible.



Entrepreneurship support program

The survey of program graduates shows that there is interest among young adult graduates in starting their own businesses, which indicates that there might be high take-up for any entrepreneurship training/financing/support program. However, a review of past programs suggests that the program that targeted youth in the last years of high school was not viable.


Job matching program

Recently a pre-pilot of a program attempted to connect the young adult graduates to a Servicio Nactional de Empleo (SNE) or a National Employment Service, a job board for formal employment specific for high school graduates, was implimented. Due to low take-up of the services, the program was discontinued. The survey of program graduates indicates that older graduates spend much longer time searching for jobs, suggesting that future program should also target older graduates.


Mentorship and work preparation program

While previous studies suggest some positive results from job training program for disadvantaged youths, there has been few similar programs in Mexico. The survey of program graduates suggests that the relationship between training and wages as well as job formality) are both positive. However, the survey provides have limited information on the training programs that they received making it difficult to draw any decisive conclusions from this portion of the analysis.
